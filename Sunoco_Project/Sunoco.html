Problem

Sunoco needed to accurately predict "High Demand" days to optimize inventory and staffing. A standard accuracy score wasn't enough; the business cost of a False Positive (missing out on oil sales) was high, but missing a True Positive (overbuying when demand fell) was worse. The goal was to maximize the True Positive to False Positive (TP/FP) ratio.

Approach

  • Model Selection: Evaluated Random Forest, XGBoost, and CatBoost. CatBoost yielded the best stability.
  • Target Engineering: defined "High Demand" dynamically as volume above the rolling 70th percentile to account for seasonal shifts.
  • Feature Engineering: Created 52-week lag features to capture annual seasonality and included macroeconomic indicators like the "Crack Spread."
  • Validation: Used TimeSeriesSplit to respect the temporal order of data during training.

Results

  • Successfully broke the client's benchmark of a 2.0 TP/FP ratio. (ended with a 4.5)

Vizualizations

  • Covid Shock: COVID significantly impacted oil demand, as it had a broad effect on the entire economy and travel. It was crucial for our model to take this into account. We did this by incorporating a binary indicator variable for the pandemic period into our feature set.
  • COVID x The Economy: COVID didn't just impact oil demand, it literally impacted every facet of the economy. This is why creating a new variable accounting for covid was so important.
  • Wild Swings in Demand by Year Depending on the year, the amount of "high-demand" oil weeks would vary wildly. This is why we ultimately ended up creating a variable that had a rolling 52 week average of "high-demand" weeks.
