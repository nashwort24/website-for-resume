Sunoco Consulting — Demand Prediction

0.60

Problem

Sunoco needed to accurately predict "High Demand" days to optimize inventory and staffing. A standard accuracy score wasn't enough; the business cost of a False Positive (staffing up for no demand) was high, but missing a True Positive (understaffing during a rush) was worse. The goal was to maximize the True Positive to False Positive (TP/FP) ratio.

Approach

  • Model Selection: Evaluated Random Forest, XGBoost, and CatBoost. CatBoost yielded the best stability.
  • Target Engineering: defined "High Demand" dynamically as volume above the rolling 70th percentile to account for seasonal shifts.
  • Feature Engineering: Created 52-week lag features to capture annual seasonality and included macroeconomic indicators like the "Crack Spread."
  • Validation: Used TimeSeriesSplit to respect the temporal order of data during training.

Results

  • Successfully broke the client's benchmark of a 2.8 TP/FP ratio.
  • Implemented a conservative probability threshold (0.60) to filter out noise and ensure high confidence in "High Demand" predictions.

Problem

The sinking of the Titanic is one of the most infamous shipwrecks in history. While there was some element of luck involved in surviving, some groups of people were more likely to survive than others. The challenge is to build a predictive model that answers the question: “what sorts of people were more likely to survive?”

Approach

  • Data Cleaning: Handled missing values for Age (imputation) and Cabin features.
  • EDA: Visualized survival rates by Gender, Class, and Age groups using Seaborn.
  • Modeling: Trained a Random Forest Classifier to predict survival binary (0/1).

Results

  • Achieved a Kaggle accuracy score of 0.79.
  • Confirmed that "Sex" (Female) and "Pclass" (1st Class) were the strongest determinants of survival.
